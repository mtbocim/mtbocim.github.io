A little about myself:

I've been repairing musical instrument for nearly a decade, and looking to move into the world of the web. I've been dabbling in logic and coding since high school on my ti-83.

Since then it has manifested in different places throughout my adulthood. My favorite bit of code I ever wrote was a program I wrote in college for a music theory class. It used music rules to generate a text file which was then read by a music notation program plugin I also wrote and transcribed into four distinct voices (I'm proud to say I recieved an A on that project).

In my downtime, I'm 100% a hobbyist, occasional jazz performer, cad drawing to 3d printing creator, pc and board gamer, origami folder, nature photographer.

I think I just like patterns and structure, wherever that may surface, and strive to incorporate them into my life.
